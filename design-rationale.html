Module 4 · Leading from the Side

Conflict Resolution Simulator
Design Rationale

CCA Leadership by Design · Johanna Huarachi

Purpose

Lateral leadership — influencing without authority — is the hardest leadership skill to practice because real conflicts only happen once. This simulator creates a safe space to rehearse decision points that design leaders face regularly, with immediate consequences and coaching. The goal is deliberate practice before the situation is live.

Frameworks Embedded

Five Conflict Styles Fighter · Negotiator · Diplomat · Avoider · People Pleaser. Each choice in the simulator is explicitly tagged to one of these styles so users can recognize their default patterns.
AID Feedback Model Action → Impact → Desired behavior. Woven into peer-feedback moments, particularly the critique and intake-bypass scenarios.
SBI Feedback Model Situation → Behavior → Impact. Used for upward/cross-functional feedback where observable specificity is key (accessibility scope scenario).
Radical Candor Care personally + challenge directly. Appears at decision points where the leader must be honest without being cruel — often the highest-scoring option in ambiguous situations.

Scenarios and Why I Chose Them

Scenario Core tension Why it matters
The Deadline Crunch PM wants to skip user research The most common design-PM conflict. Forces the player to balance speed vs. quality without formal authority. Teaches interest-based negotiation: what does the PM actually need vs. what they're asking for?
The Public Disagreement Peer challenges you in a critique Public conflict activates self-protection instincts. This scenario teaches venue management (move it private) and peer feedback using AID — separating the content disagreement from the process problem.
The Accessibility Cut Engineering defers a11y scope Involves a values conflict (equity/legality vs. speed) plus a recurring pattern problem. Tests whether players can hold a principled line skillfully using SBI and Radical Candor rather than just fighting.
The Intake Bypass VP goes directly to designer A structural problem that erodes team trust. Tests the player's ability to protect their team first, then address the VP — and to frame process as serving quality, not gatekeeping.

Design Decisions

  • Framework labels are visible on every choice — so players learn to recognize styles as they choose, not just in hindsight.
  • Consequences precede coaching — players see what happened before they get the analysis. This mirrors how real life works and reduces the feeling of being lectured.
  • Multiple decision points per scenario — some scenarios chain into a second decision, reinforcing that leadership situations rarely end with one choice.
  • No "right answer" gating — players can explore every branch. The simulator rewards curiosity, not just the optimal path.
  • Scores are shown as effectiveness, not grades — the language is "Highly effective" or "Mixed outcome" rather than pass/fail, which reflects the course's context-first framing.

What I'd Add with More Time

  • A personal debrief: which conflict style did the player use most, and what does that pattern suggest?
  • A "replay with hindsight" mode that shows all options simultaneously after completion.
  • A scenario for managing up — navigating when your own manager is the source of unclear direction.
